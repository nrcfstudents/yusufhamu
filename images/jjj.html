    Dear Kadra,

    How are you doing? I hope you are doing fine as I am. How is everyone doing? We are also doing fine. I hope you enjoyed this holiday as I did and that you are still enjoying it. Everything is going fine, but my mother was benched in work and now we have to change our living standards. The life we were used to is now changing and it is becoming difficult to adapt to the new life.

    My brother finally got a job and he is now helping our mother. The problem is the job he got is in Tanzania and we won't see him again until 2026. We are going to miss him, but my mother said it was for his own good. She said that it will teach him on how to live with others away from home and know how to live without her supervision. Even though he is far, we still talk through the phone and make jokes all the time. I will miss the annoying kin and friendly physical brother that I have known.

    School is going on well and it has made many changes. From school we are advised not to engage on online business until we finish school. They say that they have gotten reports of children dropping out of school in order to concentrate on those sites. They say that we should not waste our parents' money all for nothing.

    Although my grades have dropped, I still try my best to catch up with others. After dropping subjects, I finally found History being my best subject because it tells us of things we didn't know about. I have engaged on Music Festival, but we failed to go to nationals level and managed the fifth position at the regional level which was the door to nationals level. Even though we failed, members of our school proceeded and went to represent us in the nationals level which was held at Eldoret county. We wish them all the luck and hope for their prosperity.

    The lessons I learnt here helped me even in school to choose the correct friends and now they are helping me practice academic work while we are at home. It helped me get to know the effects of drugs and help many people stop in taking or using drugs. I helped one of my friends to stop taking alcohol. That's what has been going on in my life for now. I hope to hear from you soon. Thank you for sponsoring me and I wish you a happy long life like for your gratitude and I wish to be like you someday so that I can also help needy children. Thank you, and goodbye.

    Yours faithfully,

    Yusuf Hamu

    Dear Kadra Abdi,

    How are you doing?. I am also doing fine and I hope you and your family are also doing fine. How was your new year? mine was fine and I finally advanced to form two. I just closed the end of term one at the beginning of this month and now I am remaining with one week we re-open school. And I cant wait for term two. How are you doing there? I hope you are all doing as good as I am. It has been a long holiday but it was good enough for I got to see my family again

    Our closing was not expected but at least I got home safe and sound without any injuries. My little brother switched places now he is living with my aunt at Machakos where there are many of his own age. when I got home I was surprised to find the house empty. This was because we had shifted areas. We don't live far from our previous home but the living conditions have increased due to enough rent money provided by three family members. Where we live now is somewhere we have a water source me and my brothers own bedroom. It is a place where when you open the door no one from the road can see the inside of your house and the toilet is inside the house. This is all because of you. If you would have not sponsored me then my mother would have issues with paying my school fees and paying the rent.

    Life at school is going fine. I am now officially a member of the music festival Muslim crew club and we are preparing for the competitions that will take place when we open school. I was participating in javelin. During drama festivals our school film also reached the national level. This is all due to our new school principal, He even made for the school a bakery and even made the school accept their first ever fest that took place in one of the national schools in Machakos county. In school I have been able to improve in History and Geography even though our teacher hasn't taught us since form one. I was able to score a grade of B.

    Back to the principal, he even made the prefects go on their own trip. We hope he stays in the school for a long time. The BAD news from the school is that one of our dorms burnt to ash due to electrical wires snapping and landing on one of the beds. The dorm was home to two hundred and thirty six students. We thank God no one got hurt. other than that, Ramadhan was long and was tough mainly the days at school but I was lucky to finish it safe and sound.

    Eid was fine, and I hope yours was as good as mine. My aunt came together with my cousins and brother and we went to a recreational area. I will try to continue with improving in my grades. This days at home am woken up at four in the morning so that I can study so when I go back to school I am already used to the system of waking up at that time. I am always applying what I am taught with both teachers in school and NRCF in my day to day life and they are working very well. They have helped me upgrade in my grades even if it is a little. I believe in the sayin "A journey of a thousand miles starts with a single step".

    Me going to school is all thanks to you and I shall pray for Allah to open your way further and help you change more lives in this world thank you for sponsoring me and I wish you a long lasting satisfied life in the world. I hope one day I will come to repay you for your gratitude thank you have a lovely end month, Bye.

    Your son,

    YUSUF HAMU.
